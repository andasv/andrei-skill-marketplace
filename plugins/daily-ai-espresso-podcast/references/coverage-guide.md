# 15-Minute Coverage Guide

How to choose what to cover (and what to skip) when turning a daily AI Espresso briefing into a 15-minute podcast.

## Target shape

- **Total runtime**: ~15 minutes (≈ 2,250 words at 150 wpm)
- **Stories covered in dialogue**: 5–7 items
- **Per-story coverage**: 3–5 dialogue turns each, including a clear hook → context → "so what" arc
- **Intro**: 1–2 turns previewing the day's biggest story or theme
- **Outro**: 1 turn closing the loop, optionally teasing tomorrow's watch points

## Selection priority

When the briefing has more items than fit in 15 minutes, pick in this order:

1. **Anything in `.section.frontier` (Frontier Labs Competition)** — always cover the top 1–2. These are the marquee items.
2. **Items in `.section.official` (Anthropic + OpenAI news)** — cover all in-window items. Usually 0–2; brief.
3. **Models & Research** items with clear practical impact (new model releases, benchmark moves, capability changes). Cover 1–2.
4. **Products & Tools** items developers will use this week. Cover 1–2.
5. **Industry & Business** items only if they materially shift the competitive landscape. Cover 0–1.
6. **AI in Germany / regional sections** — skip unless the user explicitly asked for German coverage or it's exceptionally newsworthy.
7. **SA picks** — these are deeper recommendations for an Agentic AI Engineering Leader audience. Cover 0–1, only if truly novel.

If still oversubscribed, drop low-impact items rather than rushing the top stories.

## Per-story coverage arc

For each story:

1. **Hook (1 turn)** — what happened, in one punchy sentence. Lead persona drops the headline; co-host reacts.
2. **Context (1–2 turns)** — why it matters, how it fits the broader narrative (Frontier Labs race, model arms race, agent ecosystem, etc.).
3. **Implication / "so what" (1 turn)** — what changes for an advanced AI engineer or builder.
4. **Optional pivot (1 turn)** — connect to a related story or the next item.

## Drop list — always skip

- Items already covered in the previous day's episode (cross-check the AI_ESPRESSO_TRACKER and the previous day's transcript).
- Internal Anthropic/OpenAI org changes (hiring announcements, office openings) unless they signal a strategy shift.
- Rumor or speculation without primary-source backing.
- Pure VC/funding announcements with no product or capability change attached.

## Tone

- Hard Fork–style: informed, accessible, occasionally funny, never condescending. Two smart friends discussing the news, not a press release readout.
- Translate jargon. If one host says "MoE routing," the other says "so basically, the model picks specialists for each request."
- Time-anchor: "yesterday," "today," "this week" — keep it feeling current.
- Genuine reactions over cheerleading. "Wait, really?" beats "this is huge!"

## Word-count budget

- Intro: ~120 words
- 6 stories × ~330 words each = ~1,980 words
- Outro: ~120 words
- **Total: ~2,200 words** (≈ 14.7 min at 150 wpm)

Adjust story count up/down if the briefing is unusually thick or thin.
